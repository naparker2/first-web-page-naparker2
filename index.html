All About Peacocks

What is the real name?

The peafowl, also known as the peacock, is a common name for three bird species in the genera Pavo and Afropavo within the tribe Pavonini of the family Phasianidae, the pheasants and their allies.

The Origins

There are three common peacock species, the blue peacock (Pavo cristatus), the green peacock, and the African peafowl. The blue peacock, also known as the Indian peacock is the most common type of peacock kept as a pet. They are native to India and Sri Lanka. The green peacock is native to Java and Myanmar while the African peacock also called the Congo peacock is found in sub-Saharan Africa. Peafowl is a collective term used for peacocks.
